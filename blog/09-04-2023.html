Knowledge (Epistemology)

I believe most people have an inner mologue. For me, my inner mologue is in English. For my parents (Chinese immigrents), I can guess at some point it was mostly Chinese with some English.

What does it mean to know something? Assume it is sufficient to know something if the knower can explain it to a understander in objective words.

Example

Alice says to Bob: "Two plus two equals four"

There are a 4 cases here:

Alice did know 2+2=4. Bob did know 2+2=4. Both parties know 2+2=4. No new knowledge has been shared.

Alice did know 2+2=4. Bob did not know 2+2=4. Bob now has the words necessary to explain 2+2=4 to a new person, therefore Bob now knows 2+2=4.

Alice did not know 2+2=4. Bob may or may not have known 2+2=4. Either way, Alice has shared the words necessary to prove she knows 2+2=4. Both parties in this context know 2+2=4.

Example

Alice says to Bob: "The color red is warm and apples are red."

Not all apples are red and some hot temperatures show blue. Alice would have failed to explain the color red to Bob.

Example

Alice says to Bob: "The color red is your eyes perceiving photons vibrating at frequency X."

Should be good enough.

Goal

Something something, the end goal here may be: A fact bank + a random generative model, may be sufficient to rift upon and prove new truthy ideas.

Information

The internet is an invention for humans to share information quickly.

There should exist some function that represents the value of information.

v(i,t) = $ value of information i at time t

v(i,t) = c(i,t) + p(i,t)

c(i,t) = $ cost of transfering information i at time t (internet highways cost money)

p(i,t) = $ an information premium at time t (passwords are more valuable than lorem ipsum!)

Goal

We may be able to generalize the cost of all information.
